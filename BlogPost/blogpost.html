Georgian Asset Declarations

Transparency International Georgia created a database consisting of all information contained in Asset Declarations by Georgian Public Officials. These are available in PDF format from https://declaration.gov.ge/eng/. An example of such of an Asset Declaration is one by Bidzina Ivanishvili. In this blogpost we report on the content and the amount of information that is available in the database of Asset Declarations. The database is freely available upon request from TI Georgia.

From data about separate individuals to data about groups

The format and the manner in which the data is presented at the Government website is person-centric: we can view the answers to all questions of one person together in one file. That is convenient if we are interested in that one person, but less if we want to compare persons. For that reason we collected all information from the Asset Declarations and grouped it per question.

Turning the data 90 degrees

Originally we had 11 thousand files each containing data about one person for 11 different questions. Now we have 11 files containing data of all persons: one spreadsheet for each question. This makes group analysis possible. As an example, consider the question
What is the ratio of man and women working in each ministery?
We can answer that by taking all Asset Declarations filed in one year, group them by Ministery, and count how many of them are by Males and how many by Females. If we order the ministeries by the percentages of females working there we get the following figure. (Click here for larger figure) The only ministry in which the majority is female (Ministry of Culture and Monument Protection of Georgia) is quite small: 9 females and 8 males. The Ministry of Internal Affairs of Georgia has the smallest percentage of women working there and by far the largest number of people (946 males and 62 females). Description of the dataset

We know describe the dataset. It is available at data.transparancy.ge/AssetDeclarations

Size of the data

Late November 2013 we could collect 11.125 Asset Declarations (AD's), which were submitted by 5.182 different people. We found a maximum of 6 AD's submitted by one person. One of these was Tariel Fanchulidze. Each Asset Declaration consists of information about the person filing the declaration plus answers in table format to 11 questions. List of questions

ADpaid_work Have you or your family members undertaken any type of paid work in Georgia or abroad, except for working in an enterprise?

AD_gifts Have you or your family members received any gifts valued at more than 500 GEL (300 USD approximately) during the reporting period (1 January - 31 December)?
ADincome_or_expenditures Have you or your family members received any income or had any expenditures during the reporting period (1January - 31 December) valued at more than 1,500 GEL (900 USD approximately) that you did not indicate on the pages above?

Schema of our data base

The following table lists the schema for each spreadsheet corresponding to a question. Note that the number of columns in the spreadsheet is often larger than the number of columns in the PDF's. This is because we separate information like Name into two columns First Name, Last Name. We also separate texts like 10000.00 GEL or 800 m2 into two columns. Each line in each spreadsheet also contains the unique number of the Asset Declaration it came from (here called "AssetDeclarationID"). File Formats

The data is available in both English and Georgian, in both CSV and XML format. The information in CSV and XML is exactly the same. The information for English and Georgian is the same, except for the question about entrepeneurial activities. In that file the English version contains more data.
